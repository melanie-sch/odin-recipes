Snickerdoodles

Description

Snickerdoodle cookies are a variation on the classic sugar cookie, known for their cinnamon sugar coating and tangy flavor. We're not sure how snickerdoodle cookies got their name. One guess is that it orignated from "schneckennudel", a German word for "sweet bun".

These cookies only take a half an hour to make, using ingredients that are already in your kitchen. If you're looking for a short and sweet baking project (pun intended), give these snickerdoodle cookies a try!

Ingredients

Cookie dough:

  • White sugar: 1 1/2 cups
  • Butter, softened: 1 cup
  • Eggs: 2 eggs
  • Vanilla extract: 1 teaspoon
  • All-purpose flour: 2 3/4 cups
  • Cream of tartar: 2 teaspoons
  • Baking soda: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1/2 teaspoon

Cinnamon sugar coating:

  • White sugar: 1/3 cups
  • Cinnamon: 2 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. Mix cookie dough. Beat sugar, butter, eggs, and vanilla extract in a large bowl.
  3. Add dry ingredients. Whisk flour, cream of tartar, baking soda, and salt together in a separate bowl. Gradually mix dry ingredients mixture into the wet ingredients.
  4. Form the cookies. Shape the cookie dough into balls.
  5. Mix cinnamon sugar coating. Combine sugar and cinnamon in a small bowl.
  6. Coat the cookies. Place dough balls in cinnamon sugar mixture and roll until coated. Place balls 2 inches apart on ungreased baking sheets.
  7. Bake for 8 to 10 minutes. For firmer cookies, bake an extra 1 to 2 minutes.
  8. Remove from the oven. Let the cookies sit for a few minutes, then transfer to cooling racks.
  9. Enjoy!
